Understanding POTS: Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome

POTS is a form of dysautonomia that affects the body's automatic functions, particularly blood flow regulation when changing positions. Learn about its symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment options.

What is POTS?

Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS) is a condition that affects the autonomic nervous system, which controls involuntary body functions like heart rate, blood pressure, and digestion.

When a person with POTS stands up, their heart rate increases abnormally, often accompanied by symptoms such as lightheadedness, fatigue, and brain fog.

Affects Blood Flow

POTS impacts how blood flows through the body, especially when changing positions.

Autonomic Dysfunction

The condition involves dysfunction of the autonomic nervous system.

Common Symptoms

Cardiovascular

  • Rapid heartbeat upon standing
  • Chest pain or palpitations
  • Low blood pressure

Neurological

  • Brain fog and difficulty concentrating
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness

General

  • Chronic fatigue
  • Exercise intolerance
  • Temperature regulation issues

Diagnosis & Testing

Diagnostic Criteria

Tilt Table Test

A specialized test that monitors heart rate and blood pressure changes while tilting a patient from lying to standing position.

Blood Tests

Various blood tests to rule out other conditions and check for underlying causes.

Medical History

Detailed review of symptoms, triggers, and family history.

Important Notes

  • Diagnosis can be challenging and may take time
  • Multiple tests may be necessary
  • Symptoms can vary significantly between patients

Treatment Approaches

Medications

  • Beta blockers
  • Fludrocortisone
  • Midodrine

Lifestyle Changes

  • Increased fluid intake
  • Salt supplementation
  • Graduated exercise program

Support Measures

  • Compression garments
  • Physical therapy
  • Psychological support
